How are the poorest people affected by Trump's proposed Budget Cuts in HUD?

In mid-March, Trump announced his "skinny budget" for the 2018 fiscal year including a 13% budget cut to the Department of Housing and Urban Development. One of the programs that he has propsed cutting entirely are the Community Development Block Grants (CDBG). These grants are used by states, counties, and towns to fund a wide variety or programs such as affordable housing, childcare support, equipping fire stations, and more.

To break this complex funding source down, we are going to explore CDBG funding in the state of Pennsylvania based on the most recent data set, which is from 2014.

What is CDBG?


Community Development Block Grants (CDBG) are a funding source used by states, counties, and towns. Applicants must show, either through census data, or polling of specific recipients, that the majority of the beneficiaries are either extremely low income or low income. These grants are used to fund projecs such as sidewalks, childcare centers, aid in affordable housing projects, homeless facilities, and much, much more. Who benefits from CDBG


This flow diagram, or sankey chart, shows how the funding from CDBG is distributed: first to the program type, then to each county in the state of Pennsylvania, and finally to different levels of income within the state demographics. In order to qualify for funding from Community Block Development Grants, the majority (>50%) must be either in the extremely low income and low income range for that county. All grant recipients record where the project is located, who benefits, and the income levels for that area.
