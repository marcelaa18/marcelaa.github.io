Trump's biggest budget cuts in HUD affect everyone in Pennsylvania

In March of 2017, Trump announced his 2018 "skinny budget" including a 13% budget cut to the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD). One of the programs that he has proposed cutting entirely is the Community Development Block Grants (CDBG). We explore CDBG funding in the state of Pennsylvania due to recent election results as a swing state.

Find Out How

Biggest cuts in housing come from CDBG

From the 31 programs in HUD, Trump is proposing to cut three programs completely: CDBG, HOME, and Choice Neighborhoods. These three programs add up to $4 billion, $3 billion of which come from CDBG. The diagram below represents the entire HUD budget from President Obama's last term in office. As CDBG is the largest grant source being cut (shown in red), we have decided to take a closer look at what will be lost if this program is dismantled.

CDBG funds a mix of housing and public infrastructure


Community Development Block Grants (CDBG) are a funding source used by states, counties, and towns*. These grants are used to fund public facilities improvements like sidewalks, parks, child-care centers neighborhood rehabilitation and disaster relief. It also provides public services for youth and seniors, crime awareness, micro-enterprise assistance and more. To see what types of projects are funded by CDBG, take a closer look at the diagram below. 

The inner circle of the chart represents the total funding for all of the counties in Pennsylvania in 2017. CDBG are distributed within seven different categories (Housing, Public Facilities, Public Services, etc.) The middle ring shows how the funds are distributed between these categories. Low income sectors lose most with Trump's Budget Cuts


This flow diagram, or sankey chart, shows how the funding from CDBG is distributed: first to the program type, then to each county in Pennsylvania, and finally to different levels of income within the state demographics. In order to qualify for funding from Community Block Development Grants, the majority (>50%) must be either in the extremely low income and low income range for that county.

Conclusions

Both rural and urban areas will be affected by Trump's HUD budget cuts. Urban areas receive more funding, but poorer people in the extremely low income category will be impacted the most. Although may not collectively receive as much funding as cities, they have fewer funding if CDBG is elimiated than urban areas so may ultimately may suffer more.
